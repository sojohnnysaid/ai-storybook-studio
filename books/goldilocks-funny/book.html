Goldilocks & the Three Bears

Home Security Edition

A cautionary tale about home invasion
and the importance of good locks

Meet the Bears

Once upon a time, in a cozy cottage deep in the woods, there lived three bears. But these were no ordinary bears. These were smart bears. Prepared bears. Bears who had read every online review for every home security system on the market.

There was great big Papa Bear, who worked in cybersecurity and took home defense very seriously. There was Mama Bear, who had a concealed-carry permit and could field-strip a water pistol in under thirty seconds. And there was wee little Baby Bear, who mostly just wanted to watch cartoons.

Their cottage had fourteen security cameras, motion-activated floodlights, a smart doorbell with facial recognition, and a Wi-Fi network so powerful that birds avoided flying over the house.

• REC

One morning, Mama Bear made a big pot of porridge — organic, locally sourced, fair-trade oats — and ladled it into three bowls.

Papa Bear “This porridge is too hot. Let’s go for a walk while it cools. I’ll monitor the house from my phone.”

Mama Bear “Honey, we live in the middle of a forest. Who’s going to break in?”

Papa Bear “That’s EXACTLY what someone with no security system would say.”

Baby Bear “Can I bring my scooter?”

And so the three bears headed out for a walk, Papa Bear clutching his phone like a lifeline, checking the security feeds every forty-five seconds.

Papa Bear couldn’t relax. Every squirrel that crossed the yard triggered a notification. Every gust of wind that rattled the smart doorbell sent him into high alert.

Mama Bear “Would you put that phone away? You’re going to walk into a tree.”

Papa Bear “That’s what the trees WANT you to think.”

Baby Bear picked dandelions. Mama Bear took deep, calming breaths. Papa Bear zoomed in on a suspicious-looking mushroom.

And then…

Meanwhile, on a nearby forest path…
INTRUDER ALERT!

Papa Bear’s phone exploded with notifications.

MOTION DETECTED — FRONT DOOR
MOTION DETECTED — KITCHEN
MOTION DETECTED — LIVING ROOM
UNKNOWN FACE DETECTED
PORRIDGE TAMPER ALERT

Papa Bear “I KNEW IT! I KNEW THIS WOULD HAPPEN! THERE’S SOMEONE IN OUR HOUSE!”

He fumbled for his phone, paws shaking, and dialed 911.

Papa Bear “Forest Police? I’d like to report a BREAK-IN. Yes, I have it on fourteen cameras. FOURTEEN.”

Then he called Mama Bear.

The Call

Now, what Papa Bear didn’t know was that Mama Bear and Baby Bear had turned back early. Baby Bear had gotten a pebble in his shoe and Mama didn’t feel like dealing with the whining. They were already home — upstairs in the bedroom — when Papa’s call came through.

Mama Bear’s phone buzzed. She answered.

Papa Bear “There’s an intruder downstairs. GET STRAPPED.”

Mama Bear’s eyes went wide. Then they went narrow. Then they went full mama bear.

She walked to the bedroom closet, pressed her paw to the biometric scanner, and the BearVault 3000 slid open with a dramatic hiss.

“Baby Bear, stay in your room. Mama’s got this.”

Meanwhile, downstairs…

• REC — KITCHEN CAM 03

Goldilocks was having the absolute best day of her life.

She had wandered into the most darling little cottage, and nobody seemed to be home! How fun! What an adventure!

She did not notice the fourteen security cameras. She did not notice the blinking red lights. She did not notice the motion-sensor floodlight that had turned on so bright you could see it from space.

She tried Papa Bear’s porridge.

“Too hot!”

She tried Mama Bear’s porridge.

“Too cold!”

She tried Baby Bear’s porridge.

“Mmmmm! Just right!”

She ate every last bite, completely unaware that she was being filmed from four different angles.

• REC — LIVING ROOM CAM 07
The Three Chairs

With a full tummy and not a care in the world, Goldilocks bounced into the living room.

She tried Papa Bear’s enormous recliner.

“Too hard! And why does it have a cup holder for binoculars?”

She tried Mama Bear’s chair.

“Too soft! But this throw blanket is divine.”

She tried Baby Bear’s wee little chair.

“Just right!”

CRACK! SNAP! CRASH!

The chair exploded into splinters. Goldilocks tumbled to the floor, giggled, brushed herself off, and headed upstairs.

Camera 07 captured the whole thing in stunning 4K.

An Unexpected Guest

Goldilocks skipped upstairs, tried Papa Bear’s bed (“Too hard!”), tried Mama Bear’s bed (“Too soft!”), and then opened the door to Baby Bear’s room.

And there, sitting on his little bed in his pajamas, clutching his teddy bear with eyes the size of dinner plates, was Baby Bear.

“OH MY GOODNESS! Hello! You are SO CUTE!”

Baby Bear “M-m-mama…?”

FREEZE!

From behind came a voice that could freeze lava.

“HANDS UP. DON’T. MOVE.”

Goldilocks turned around slowly. There stood Mama Bear in the doorway, feet planted wide, looking like she had just stepped out of an action movie — if action movies starred bears in cozy sweaters.

“Oh! Um… this isn’t what it looks like?”

“It looks like you ate my son’s porridge, broke his chair, and now you’re in his bedroom. That’s EXACTLY what it looks like.”

BUSTED!

By the time Mama Bear marched Goldilocks downstairs with her hands up, Papa Bear had arrived with backup.

And what backup it was.

Officer Peppy Le Stink of the Forest Police Department stood at attention by the front door, adjusting his badge with great dignity. His partner, Officer Buck Chompsworth, a beaver of considerable girth, was already scribbling furiously in a tiny notepad.

Officer Chompsworth “So let me get this straight. She ate the porridge. She broke the chair. And she was found in the minor’s bedroom?”

Papa Bear “AND I HAVE IT ALL ON CAMERA!”

Read Her Her Rights

Officer Le Stink placed a pair of comically tiny handcuffs on Goldilocks and led her toward the Forest PD cruiser.

Officer Le Stink “You have the right to remain silent. Anything you say can and will be used against you in a court of forest law.”

Goldilocks “But the door was unlocked!”

Officer Chompsworth “Ma’am, that’s not how property law works.”

Papa Bear waved from the porch, grinning like a bear who had just been proven right about everything, ever, in the history of being right.

Mama Bear “I will admit… the cameras were a good idea.”

Papa Bear “I’m sorry, could you say that louder? For the recording?”

The End

The bears installed four more cameras that very afternoon. Baby Bear got a new chair — with a built-in alarm. And Goldilocks? She learned a very important lesson:

If it’s not your house, don’t go in.
Especially if there are cameras.
