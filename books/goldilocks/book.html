Goldilocks & the Three Bears

A Timeless Tale Retold

Illustrated with the magic of imagination

1
Once Upon a Time

Once upon a time, in a cozy little cottage tucked deep within an enchanted forest, there lived three bears. There was great big Papa Bear, whose voice rumbled like gentle thunder. There was middle-sized Mama Bear, whose voice was warm as honey. And there was wee little Baby Bear, whose voice was as small and sweet as a tinkling bell.

They were the happiest family in all the woodland, and every creature in the forest — from the tiniest field mouse to the wisest old owl — loved them dearly.

Their cottage had round windows that glowed like lanterns at dusk, a garden bursting with sunflowers, and a chimney that always trailed the most delicious smells into the treetops above.

2

One bright and golden morning, Mama Bear stirred a big pot of creamy porridge over the crackling fire. The kitchen filled with the sweet smell of oats and honey, swirling through the cottage like a warm hug.

She ladled the porridge into three bowls — a great big bowl for Papa Bear, a middle-sized bowl for herself, and a wee little bowl for Baby Bear — and set them on their old wooden table.

Papa Bear “This porridge is far too hot to eat!”

Mama Bear “Let us take a walk in the forest while it cools.”

Baby Bear “Oh yes! Perhaps we’ll see the rabbits!”

And so the three bears set off along the winding forest path, leaving their front door unlocked — for in those days, no one in the forest ever locked their doors.

The bears ambled happily along the mossy path. Papa Bear pointed out interesting mushrooms. Mama Bear gathered wildflowers for the kitchen table. Baby Bear chased butterflies and splashed in every puddle he could find.

But while the three bears were away, someone else was wandering through the very same forest…

A little girl named Goldilocks came skipping through the trees. She had the most magnificent golden curls that bounced and shimmered in the dappled sunlight, and eyes as bright and curious as a magpie’s.

When she spotted the bears’ cottage, with its round windows and curling chimney smoke, she stopped and stared.

“What a perfectly lovely little house!”

3
The Three Porridges

Goldilocks knocked on the door. When no one answered, she pushed it open and stepped inside. The kitchen smelled absolutely heavenly.

On the table sat three bowls of porridge, and Goldilocks — who had not eaten breakfast — felt her tummy rumble.

She picked up the big spoon from Papa Bear’s great big bowl and took a taste.

“Ouch! This porridge is TOO HOT!”

She tried Mama Bear’s middle-sized bowl.

“Brrr! This porridge is TOO COLD!”

Then she tried Baby Bear’s wee little bowl.

“Mmmm! This porridge is JUST RIGHT!”

And she ate it all up, every last drop, until the little blue bowl was completely empty. She even licked the spoon.

4
The Three Chairs

With a full tummy and tired legs, Goldilocks wandered into the sitting room, where she found three chairs.

She climbed up into Papa Bear’s great big chair.

“This chair is TOO HARD!”

She wriggled into Mama Bear’s middle-sized chair.

“This chair is TOO SOFT!”

Then she sat in Baby Bear’s wee little chair.

“This chair is JUST RIGHT!”

But Goldilocks was a little too heavy for the wee little chair, and with a CRACK and a SNAP — it broke into pieces!

Down tumbled Goldilocks, right onto the rug.

5
The Three Beds

Feeling quite sleepy now, Goldilocks climbed the creaky stairs and found a bedroom with three beds.

She lay upon Papa Bear’s great big bed. “Too hard!” She tried Mama Bear’s middle-sized bed. “Too soft!”

Then she curled up in Baby Bear’s wee little bed…

“This bed is JUST RIGHT.”

And in no time at all, she was fast asleep.

6
The Bears Come Home

Before long, the three bears returned from their walk, feeling hungry and ready for their porridge.

Papa Bear looked at his great big bowl and growled in his great big voice:

Papa Bear “SOMEBODY HAS BEEN EATING MY PORRIDGE!”

Mama Bear looked at her middle-sized bowl and said in her middle-sized voice:

Mama Bear “Somebody has been eating my porridge!”

Baby Bear looked at his wee little bowl and cried in his wee little voice:

Baby Bear “Somebody has been eating my porridge — and they’ve eaten it ALL UP!”

The bears looked at each other with wide, worried eyes. Then they noticed the chairs…

✻ ✻ ✻
Something was very wrong in the bears’ little cottage.
Someone had come inside while they were away…
✻ ✻ ✻

In the sitting room, Papa Bear saw that his cushion had been squashed.

Papa Bear “SOMEBODY HAS BEEN SITTING IN MY CHAIR!”

Mama Bear “Somebody has been sitting in my chair!”

Baby Bear “Somebody has been sitting in my chair — and they’ve BROKEN it all to pieces!”

Poor Baby Bear’s eyes filled with tears as he looked at the pile of splintered wood that had once been his favourite little chair.

The three bears crept upstairs, their hearts beating fast. In the bedroom, Papa Bear saw that his pillow had been moved.

Papa Bear “SOMEBODY HAS BEEN SLEEPING IN MY BED!”

Mama Bear “Somebody has been sleeping in my bed!”

Then Baby Bear looked at his wee little bed, and there — with golden curls spread across his pillow — was…

Baby Bear “Somebody has been sleeping in my bed —
and SHE’S STILL HERE!”

7

Baby Bear’s little squeak woke Goldilocks with a start. She opened her eyes and saw three bears staring right at her — one VERY big, one rather big, and one very small indeed.

✻ ✻ ✻
Away She Ran!

“Oh my! Oh my! Oh MY!”

Goldilocks leapt out of the little bed, scrambled down the stairs, burst through the front door, and ran — oh, how she ran! — through the forest as fast as her legs could carry her.

She jumped over logs, ducked under branches, splashed through the stream, and didn’t stop running until the little cottage was far, far behind her, hidden among the ancient trees.

✻ ✻ ✻

The three bears watched from their doorway as the little golden-haired girl disappeared into the trees. Papa Bear scratched his head. Mama Bear shook hers. And Baby Bear?

Baby Bear just waved goodbye.

The End

The bears fixed Baby Bear’s chair with a little glue and a lot of love. They always locked their door after that — but they left a little bowl of porridge on the porch, just in case a hungry traveller ever passed by again.

And Goldilocks? She never forgot the lesson she learned that day: always ask before you borrow, and never, ever sit in a chair that’s too small for you.
